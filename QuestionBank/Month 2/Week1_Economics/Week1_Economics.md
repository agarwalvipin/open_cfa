A firm is experiencing a situation where its average revenue is greater than its average variable cost but less than its average total cost. In the short run, this firm should:

- A) Shut down immediately to minimize losses.
- B) Continue to operate.
- C) Increase its price to cover average total cost.

<details>
<summary>âœ… Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:**

* **B is correct** because in the short run, a firm should continue to operate as long as its average revenue (AR) or price (P) is greater than its average variable cost (AVC). * **A is incorrect** because shutting down immediately is the optimal decision only if average revenue is less than average variable cost. If AR < AVC, operating would mean losses exceed total fixed costs.
* **C is incorrect** because while increasing price to cover average total cost (ATC) would lead to zero economic profit or a profit, the firm may not have the market power to unilaterally increase its price, especially if it operates in a competitive market. The immediate operational decision is based on whether variable costs are covered.
