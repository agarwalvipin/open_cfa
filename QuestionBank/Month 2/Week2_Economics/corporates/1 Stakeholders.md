## 🟢 Q1 – Corp Issuers – Stakeholders  
<details><summary>Meta Data</summary>

- id: CI_L1_R21_Q1  
- reading: 21  
- los: 21.a – Compare the financial claims and motivations of lenders and shareholders.  
- difficulty: easy  
- tags: [Lenders, Shareholders]  
</details>

Which statement best describes the **primary financial claim** of a company’s lenders compared with its shareholders?

- A) Lenders hold a residual claim on profits, whereas shareholders earn fixed interest.  
- B) Lenders receive fixed contractual payments, whereas shareholders hold the residual claim on earnings.  
- C) Both groups receive residual claims but shareholders receive them first.

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B**

| Choice | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| **A** | **Incorrect.** This reverses reality. Residual profits belong to shareholders, not lenders; lenders earn fixed interest. |
| **B** | **Correct.** Debt contracts promise lenders fixed coupons/principal, while shareholders receive what is left after all obligations—i.e., the residual claim. |
| **C** | **Incorrect.** Only shareholders have residual claims; lenders must be paid first in liquidation. |
</details>

- id: CI_L1_R21_Q7  
- los: 21.a  
- difficulty: medium  
- tags: [Debt Covenants, Agency Conflict]  
</details>

Which provision **most directly** aligns the interests of lenders with shareholders when management pursues high-risk projects?

- A) A poison-pill clause  
- B) A negative debt covenant restricting leverage increases  
- C) A staggered board structure

<details>
<summary>✅ Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B**

| Choice | Analysis |
|--------|----------|
| **A** | **Incorrect.** Poison pills defend against hostile takeovers; they do not address risk-shifting toward debtholders. |
| **B** | **Correct.** By limiting new leverage, the covenant prevents management from increasing default risk, protecting lenders and aligning them with shareholders’ long-term value goals. |
| **C** | **Incorrect.** Staggered boards slow takeovers but do not constrain risk profile. |
</details>
