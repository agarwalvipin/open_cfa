- A) correctly identifies that tactical adjustments are always ineffective.
- B) fails to recognize that while specific event timing is unpredictable, many geopolitical risks are knowable and their potential impacts can be analyzed.
- C) overestimates the benefits of strategic asset allocation in mitigating all forms of risk.

<details>
<summary>âœ… Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:**

* **B is correct.** This statement presents a false dilemma. While predicting the exact timing and nature of a specific event (a "black swan") is impossible, many geopolitical trends and pressure points are well-known and can be analyzed (e.g., rising US-China competition, climate change impacting a region). Prudent risk management involves analyzing these known, high-impact "gray rhino" risks and positioning the portfolio to be resilient to a range of potential outcomes, even if the specific trigger is unpredictable. Ignoring them entirely is a dereliction of duty.
* **A is incorrect.** The statement wrongly concludes that tactical adjustments are always ineffective. While difficult, tactical shifts (e.g., reducing exposure to a high-risk region) can be a valid part of managing identifiable risks.
* **C is incorrect.** While strategic asset allocation is the primary driver of long-term returns, this manager's statement isn't about overestimating its benefits, but rather about using it as a justification for ignoring an entire category of material risk. The core error is the failure to engage in any form of geopolitical risk analysis at all.
