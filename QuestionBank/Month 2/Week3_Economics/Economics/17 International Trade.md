Country A can produce 100 tons of steel or 50 cars. Country B can produce 80 tons of steel or 30 cars. Which statement *best describes* the situation?

- A) Country A has an absolute advantage in both steel and cars.
- B) Country B has a comparative advantage in steel.
- C) Country A has a comparative advantage in cars.

<details>
<summary>âœ… Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: A**

**Explanation:**

* **A is correct.** Absolute advantage refers to the ability to produce a good using fewer resources (or produce more with the same resources). Since Country A can produce more of both goods (100 > 80 tons of steel, and 50 > 30 cars), it has an absolute advantage in both.
* **B is incorrect.** To determine comparative advantage, we must look at opportunity costs.
    * Country A's opportunity cost of 1 car is 2 tons of steel (100/50).
    * Country B's opportunity cost of 1 car is 2.67 tons of steel (80/30).
    * Since Country A has a lower opportunity cost for producing cars, it has the comparative advantage in cars, not steel. Therefore, Country B has a comparative advantage in steel.
* **C is incorrect.** As calculated above, Country A has a lower opportunity cost in producing cars (2 vs. 2.67 tons of steel), so it does have a comparative advantage in cars. However, statement A is the most complete and direct description of the advantage presented.

Which of the following policies would be consistent with a group of countries forming an Economic Union, but would *not* be required for a Common Market?

- A) Establishing a common external trade policy for non-members.
- B) Allowing the free movement of labor and capital among member nations.
- C) Coordinating and harmonizing economic policies, including monetary and fiscal policies.

<details>
<summary>âœ… Answer & Explanation</summary>

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:**

This question tests your knowledge of the different levels of economic integration.
1.  **Customs Union:** Removes internal barriers and establishes a common external policy.
2.  **Common Market:** Builds on a customs union and adds the free movement of factors of production (labor and capital).
3.  **Economic Union:** Builds on a common market and adds the coordination of economic policies (monetary, fiscal, social).

* **C is correct.** The coordination of economic policies is the defining feature that distinguishes an economic union from a common market. A monetary union, which is a type of economic union, would even have a common currency.
* **A is incorrect.** A common external trade policy is required for a customs union, which is a lower level of integration than both a common market and an economic union. Therefore, it's required for both.
* **B is incorrect.** The free movement of labor and capital is the key feature that defines a common market. It is also a feature of an economic union.
