
**Operating Profitability (EBIT Margin)**

$$
\text{EBIT Margin} = \frac{\text{EBIT}}{\text{Revenue}}
$$

---


**Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) Percentage**

$$
\text{COGS Percentage} = \frac{\text{COGS}}{\text{Revenue}} \times 100
$$

---


**Sales and Revenues**
$$
\text{Sales Growth Rate} = \frac{\text{Current Period Sales} - \text{Previous Period Sales}}{\text{Previous Period Sales}} \times 100
$$

---

**Gross Profit**

$$
\text{Gross Profit} = \text{Revenue} - \text{COGS}
$$

---


**Gross Profit Margin**

$$
\text{Gross Profit Margin} = \frac{\text{Gross Profit}}{\text{Revenue}} \times 100
$$

---

**Working Capital**
$$
\text{Working Capital} = \text{Current Assets} - \text{Current Liabilities}
$$

---


**Revenue Forecast**
$$
\text{Revenue Forecast} = \text{Current Revenue} \times (1 + \text{Growth Rate})
$$

---

**Revenue Forecast top-down**
$$
\text{Revenue Forecast (Top-Down)} = \text{Market Size} \times \text{Market Share}
$$

---


**prefferres stock (perpetuity)**
$$
\text{Preferred Stock Value} = \frac{\text{Dividend}}{\text{Required Rate of Return}}
$$

---

**Common Stock (Gordon Growth Model)**
$$
\text{Common Stock Value} = \frac{\text{Dividend per Share}}{\text{Required Rate of Return} - \text{Growth Rate}}
$$

---

**Two Stage Dividend Discount Model**
$$
\text{Common Stock Value} = \sum_{t=1}^{n} \frac{D_t}{(1+r)^t} + \frac{P_n}{(1+r)^n}
$$
where:

---

**ROE**
$$
\text{ROE} = \frac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Shareholder's Equity}} \times 100
$$

---

**DuPont Analysis**
$$
\text{ROE} = \text{Profit Margin} \times \text{Asset Turnover} \times \text{Equity Multiplier}
$$

---

**DuPont Analysis** (Expanded)
$$
\text{ROE} = \left( \frac{\text{Net Income}}{\text{Revenue}} \right) \times \left( \frac{\text{Revenue}}{\text{Total Assets}} \right) \times \left( \frac{\text{Total Assets}}{\text{Shareholder's Equity}} \right)
$$  

Where:

---

**price to earnings ratio (P/E)**
$$
\text{P/E Ratio} = \frac{\text{Market Price per Share}}{\text{Earnings per Share (EPS)}}
$$

**justified P/E ratio**
$$
\text{Justified P/E Ratio} = \frac{1 - b}{r - g}
$$
where:
- \( b \) = retention ratio (plowback ratio)
- \( 1 - b \) = dividend payout ratio
- \( r \) = required rate of return
- \( g \) = growth rate of dividends

---
**price to book ratio (P/B)**
$$
\text{P/B Ratio} = \frac{\text{Market Price per Share}}{\text{Book Value per Share (BVPS)}}
$$

---
**price to sales ratio (P/S)**
$$  
\text{P/S Ratio} = \frac{\text{Market Price per Share}}{\text{Sales per Share}}
$$
---
**price to cash flow ratio (P/CF)**
$$
\text{P/CF Ratio} = \frac{\text{Market Price per Share}}{\text{Cash Flow per Share}}
$$
---

**Enterprise Value (EV)**
$$
\text{EV} = \text{Market Capitalization} + \text{Total Debt} - \text{Cash and Cash Equivalents}
$$
**EV/EBITDA Ratio**
$$
\text{EV/EBITDA Ratio} = \frac{\text{Enterprise Value (EV)}}{\text{Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization (EBITDA)}}
$$

---

**CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model)**
$$
\text{Expected Return} = r_f + \beta \times (r_m - r_f)
$$
where:
- \( r_f \) = risk-free rate
- \( \beta \) = beta of the stock
- \( r_m \) = expected market return

---

*** Sustainable Growth Rate (SGR)***
$$
\text{SGR} = \text{ROE} \times b
$$
where:
- \( b \) = retention ratio (plowback ratio)
